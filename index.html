About

Hi! I am Shuo Huang, a Ph.D. candidate in Biomedical Engineering at USC. I develop methods for robust diffusion MRI and multimodal Alzheimer’s disease analysis, and explore inverse design for nanophotonics. I enjoy building clean, reproducible pipelines and sharing open-source tools.

Interests: neuroimage reconstruction (FODs with SPHARM), tractography reliability, contrastive/self-supervised pretraining, and Fourier/PDE-inspired design for metasurfaces.

Research

FOD Restoration with Diffusion Models dMRI

Addressing susceptibility-induced distortions and signal loss in FODs represented in SPHARM. Proposed a diffusion-based restoration method that respects order dependencies and improves tractography consistency.

Preprint / Code

Multimodal AD Diagnosis MRI+PET+Tabular

Integrating T1w MRI, PET, and cognitive scores via ViTs and TabPFN for data-efficient prediction, with region-level interpretability (SHAP) to highlight potential biomarkers.

MICCAI 2025

Inverse Design for Metasurfaces Photonics

Fourier/PDE-inspired parameterization and energy-efficient training for metalenses and Y‑splitters; compares component-wise vs. whole-structure optimization in 1D/2D arrays.

CLEO / ISBI

Open-Source Pipelines Code

Swin-UNETR, ALBEF, and TabPFN integrations for multimodal learning with clean configs, unit tests, and reproducible training scripts.
